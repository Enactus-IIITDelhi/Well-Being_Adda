LOVE YOURSELF

Definition of Self Love

According to Oxford's dictionary, Self-love is the feeling that your own happiness and wishes are paramount. Self-love might seem like just a new fad, but it is not just a trend. It is an essential contributor to living a healthier life with a healthier mind. Love yourself - it is easier said than done. We are all excellent at criticising ourselves, at seeing the worst in us. We tend to dwell on our worst flaws all the time and forget the positive bits. In this competitive and perfectionist world, we tend to have very high standards for ourselves. Therefore, we often need reminders to love ourselves.Self-love is not "unnecessary pampering" or self-pity. It is acknowledging your worth and rewarding yourself for the things well done.Self-love is not about forgetting or ignoring our mistakes but forgiving ourselves, learning and moving on, accepting all of our flaws and strengths, while striving to be better.

Love yourself.

What we define as self-love may vary from this definition, depending on where we are in our journey of self-love. Self-love is not a switch that can be turned on in a second. It is a process that takes time. Even in this journey, we have to be patient with ourselves, understand that we all are human and not perfect, even if we want to be. It is only when we let go of our bitter relationship with ourselves that we can finally see our potential and harness it to make it even better. We should be thankful for all that we have instead of just worrying about what we don’t. Self-love also includes treating our body well, not just with spas and milkshakes, but healthy food and exercise. The benefits of these are well known and are essential for improving our overall well-being. Taking breaks to spend time with ourselves just to relax is something we all need, and it is not something we need to feel guilty about. It is okay to prioritise ourselves and our mental health. “I don’t feel like it” is a valid reason not to do something and not an excuse.

It is alright to be not okay. We often compare ourselves to others, feel others’ problems are more significant than ours, and their achievements grander than ours. That may or may not be true, but we often forget to recognise the bias we have against ourselves. Sometimes it is better to move away from this competitive thinking and start focusing on ourselves. Everyone has problems, and we have to learn to be kind to ourselves like we are empathetic to others. We are our own best friends. So, be patient and kind to yourself as you take baby steps towards loving yourself more each day.
